## The three questions to keep in mind

1. Who should I speak to about protecting this?
2. Who is a trusted technical reviewer who can test it confidentially?
3. Who can help identify the first commercial wedge?

---

## The five things Anthony should appreciate by the end

1. **This is not just a language theory.** It is a potentially practical discovery about completed sentences, AI output, translation, and auditability.
2. **The phenomenon is real enough to investigate.** Anthony does not need to accept the whole system; he only needs to see that completion, landing, and sentence difference are observable.
3. **The A-packet is a tool for protection and validation.** It slows the sentence down, separates meaning from structure, and creates a repeatable process that can be tested.
4. **The commercial pathway could be significant.** If structural addresses survive translation and can audit AI-generated sentences, Settlement Gate could become a multilingual sentence-audit layer.
5. **The project needs the right first steps, not broad exposure.** Anthony’s role is to help move safely through protection, trusted review, and the PorterShed startup/IP/product network.

## Simplest takeaway

> This may be early, but it is not vague. It is a testable sentence-audit idea with possible global AI and translation applications, and it needs careful protection before wider exposure.
