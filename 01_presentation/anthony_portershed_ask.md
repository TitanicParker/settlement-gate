# The Ask to Anthony and PorterShed

## What I am asking Anthony for

I am asking Anthony for help to protect, test, and commercialise a discovery before exposing it too widely. The core claim is that every completed sentence has a recoverable structural address, and that faithful translations should land in the same address. If true, this becomes a multilingual sentence-auditing layer for AI, translation, legal text, policy language, and agent instructions. I need guidance on who to speak to first: IP protection, trusted technical validation, and commercial development — without giving away the discovery or its value.

## Why I am bringing this through PorterShed

I am bringing this to Anthony through PorterShed because PorterShed is exactly the kind of environment where an early, unusual, potentially high-value idea can be pressure-tested without immediately becoming overexposed. I understand PorterShed as a Galway innovation hub that can help connect early ideas to the right people: startup mentors, AI/product builders, IP advisors, funders, university contacts, and commercialisation pathways. I am not asking PorterShed to adopt the project immediately. I am asking whether Anthony can help me find the safest first route through protection, validation, and development.

## What I am not asking for yet

- I am not asking for a public launch.
- I am not asking PorterShed to validate the project as proven.
- I am not asking for broad exposure before protection.
- I am not asking for a finished product plan before technical pressure testing.
- I am not asking anyone to accept the framework without independent review.

## Useful introductions

- An IP advisor who can advise on disclosure risk, patentability, copyright, and trade-secret handling.
- A trusted AI / NLP reviewer who can pressure-test the method without public exposure.
- A translation expert who can examine address preservation across language pairs.
- A product builder who can identify the smallest useful demo.
- A commercialisation mentor who can advise on first-market wedge and buyer pain.
- A funder or investor who understands early technical risk and pre-validation IP.

## Safe next step

The safest next step is a private discussion to decide what should be protected first, what should not yet be shared publicly, and which trusted reviewer should test a limited version of the method under appropriate confidentiality.
