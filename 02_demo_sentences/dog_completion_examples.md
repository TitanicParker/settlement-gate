# Dog Completion Examples

## Failure to close

“Did you put the dog?”

This exposes structure by failing to close.

## Going past closure

“Did you put the dog out, milk?”

This exposes structure by going past closure.

## Explanatory note

The first sentence stops before it has landed. The listener waits for completion before any formal explanation is needed. The second sentence had already landed at “Did you put the dog out,” and then goes past closure by adding “milk.” Together, the examples show that sentence completion is not merely a matter of stopping; it is a structural event that can fail, land, or overrun.
