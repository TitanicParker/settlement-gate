# Galway / PorterShed Track-Disclosure Sentence Packet

## Purpose

This document creates a fresh layer from the Galway / PorterShed sentence set.

Each item is presented in the latest format:

```text
Original sentence
-> Address
-> Track-disclosure sentence
-> Shortcut disclosed
```

The original sentence is the train. The address discloses the tracks. The track-disclosure sentence makes those tracks visible in language. It is intentionally more explicit than the original sentence. It does not try to be better prose. It explains how the original sentence's meaning comes about through structure.

The track-disclosure sentence is therefore not required to reproduce the original sentence's surface structure. It is required to expose the route that the original sentence shortcuts through.

## 1. Galway City Council approved the new transport plan yesterday.

**Address:** Is / Toward / Distinction

**Track-disclosure sentence:**

Within Galway City Council's decision authority, the new transport plan is marked as approved rather than pending, rejected, or undecided; the time marker "yesterday" only bounds when that civic marking occurred, so attention lands on the plan as the focal approved object and what remains is its approved civic status.

**Shortcut disclosed:**

The ordinary sentence shortcuts through status-assignment. It sounds like a past action by the council, but the structural landing is the plan's approved standing.

# Closing Note

This format treats each original sentence as compressed structural motion.

The track-disclosure sentence makes explicit what the ordinary sentence shortcuts:

```text
what is governed,
what work is performed,
where attention resolves,
what remains,
and what rival reading is blocked.
```

The result is intentionally more tedious than ordinary prose because its purpose is not elegance. Its purpose is disclosure.

The ordinary sentence shows the train.

The track-disclosure sentence shows the rails.
