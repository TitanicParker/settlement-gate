# Settlement Gate

**Meeting page:** [Anthony / PorterShed deck and speaker notes](docs/index.html)

## Why “Settlement Gate”?

Because every completed sentence settles something.

And because not every settlement should be allowed to govern action without inspection.

The sentence lands.

The gate asks whether that landing is earned.

---

## What this repository is

Settlement Gate is an early-stage investigation into whether completed sentences have recoverable structural addresses, and whether faithful translations preserve those addresses even when the words and grammar change.

The practical possibility is a multilingual sentence-auditing layer for AI output, translation, legal text, policy language, and agent instructions.

This repository holds the working packet, demo sentences, A-packet material, resolver outputs, bilingual pressure tests, validation planning, product notes, and presentation material for the Anthony / PorterShed conversation.

Status: exploratory / working packet / not yet independently validated as a finished theory.

---

## Core working claim

The working claim is that completed sentences do not merely stop. They settle.

That settlement may be recoverable as a structural address: not just what the sentence is about, but what it has made stand, where attention has landed, and what structural work produced that landing.

A related working claim is that faithful translations should preserve the same structural landing even when the surface language changes.

---

## Why this matters

AI systems may generate tokens internally, but people inherit completed sentences.

Important sentences can grant permission, assign status, describe obligations, trigger actions, support legal decisions, guide agents, or move people from uncertainty into belief.

So the question is not only:

> What does this sentence mean?

It is also:

> What did this sentence settle, and was it entitled to settle it?

---

## Basic movement

```text
Sentence
→ A1–A6 inspection packet
→ Resolver
→ Structural address
→ Sentence receipt
```

The A-packet is not the whole theory. It is the instrument that slows a sentence down long enough for its commitments to become visible.

The receipt is not a summary. It is the audit trace of what the sentence has settled, what it governs, where it holds, and which rival reading must not win.

## Current caution

This is an early-stage working repository. It should not be treated as independent proof, a finished linguistic theory, legal advice, or a commercially validated product.

The correct posture is investigation: protect the idea, test it with trusted reviewers, record disagreements, and identify the smallest useful commercial validation path.
