# Packet Summary

This working packet gathers the Galway / PorterShed Settlement Gate demo material into one ordered document.

## Settlement Gate concept

The central investigation is whether completed sentences have recoverable structural addresses. The packet treats a completed sentence as more than a string of words or a topic-bearing statement. It asks what the sentence has settled, what that settlement governs, what structural work it performs, where its force holds, and what rival reading must be blocked.

The project is framed as exploratory. It is not presented as a finished theory, universal proof, or independently validated product.

## Completion and closure examples

The packet’s presentation script begins with two simple examples:

- “Did you put the dog?”
- “Did you put the dog out, milk?”

The first fails to close. The second goes past closure. Together they show the intuition that a sentence is not complete merely because it stops; it stops because it has become complete.

## A1-A6 packet method

The A-packet is used as an inspection instrument. It slows a sentence down through six questions:

1. What does the sentence settle as its loyal ordinary saying?
2. What object, relation, status, role, condition, field, or demand is governed?
3. What structural work does the survivor perform?
4. Where does it hold, and what bounds its reach?
5. What governing shape remains?
6. What tempting rival reading must be defeated?

The method is designed to resist collapsing too quickly into topic, grammar, or paraphrase.

## Resolver output idea

After A1-A6, the resolver assigns a structural landing in the display order:

```text
Remainder / Orientation / Operator
```

The packet explains the three axes as:

- Remainder: Is, With, Does.
- Orientation: Toward, Across, Away.
- Operator: Distinction, Expansion, Relation, Correction, Integration, Closure.

The full lattice is described as background, not as something a public README or product note needs to explain in detail.

## Bilingual English-Irish pressure test

The packet contains 20 Galway / PorterShed sentences. Items 16-20 are Irish equivalents for selected English items. The pattern notes state that five English-Irish pairs reproduce the same structural landing where the sentence function is equivalent.

This is treated as a local bilingual pressure test, not proof of universality.

## Potential application areas

The working packet points toward possible use in:

- AI output auditing.
- Translation validation.
- Legal and policy language review.
- Agent instruction safety.
- Customer support and public-sector communication.
- Sentence-level receipts for important claims or instructions.

## Caution

This is exploratory work and has not yet been independently validated. The packet should be handled as early-stage investigation material and pressure-test evidence, not as proof that the framework is complete or commercially validated.
